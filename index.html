Right Ventricular Pressures

Systolic and diastolic RV pressure estimation

RVSP via TR

RVSP = 4(TR Vmax)² + RAP

RVSP via VSD

RVSP = SBP − 4(VSD Vpeak)²

Assumes no LVOT obstruction

RVEDP via VSD

RVEDP = LVEDP − 4(VSD Vdiast)²

RVDP (no VSD)

RVDP = RAP − TS gradient

Pulmonary Artery Pressures

PASP, PAEDP, and mean PA pressure

PA Systolic Pressure (PASP)

PASP = RVSP (no PS)
PASP = RVSP − PSgradient

PA End-Diastolic Pressure (PAEDP)

PAEDP = 4(PRend-diast)² + RAP

Mean PA Pressure (mPAP) — Three Methods

Method 1 — Weighted

mPAP = ⅓PASP + ⅔PADP

Method 2 — PR peak

mPAP = 4(PRpeak)² + RAP

Method 3 — RVOT AcT

mPAP = 79 − (0.45 × RVOT AcT)

Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR)

Echo-derived and hemodynamic methods

Echo Method Abbas JACC 2003

PVR = 10 × (TR Vpeak / RVOT VTI) + 0.16

Hemodynamic Method

PVR = (mPAP − mPCWP) / CO

Left Atrial Pressure (LAP)

E/e' based estimation and MR method

E/e' Method

LAP = E/e' + 4
LAP = 1.24 × (E/e') + 1.9

⚠ Inaccurate in MS, MAC, prosthetic MV, severe MR, severe LV dysfunction (HCM)

MR Method

LAP = SBP − 4(MR Vmax)²

Left Ventricular Pressures

Systolic and diastolic LV pressure estimation

LV Systolic Pressure (LVSP)

LVSP = SBP (no AS)
LVSP = 4(MR Vpeak)² + LAP
LVSP = SBP + AS gradient

LV End-Diastolic Pressure (LVEDP)

LVEDP = DBP − 4(ARend-diast)²

Ar–A Duration Method

Ar–A > 30 ms → LVEDP > 20 mmHg (82% sens, 92% spec)
